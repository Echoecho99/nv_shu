NÜSHU

Nüshu is a syllabic script created and used exclusively by Ancient Yao women in Jiangyong Prefecture, Hunan Province, China. The women were forbidden formal education for many centuries and developed the Nüshu script in order to communicate with one another.

“Nushu”, the secret written language of women, is often written in the format of poems, letters and songs. It serves as an escape and a secret medium for woman to express their pain, sorrow and hope to another woman.

Ancient Yao women had the custom of tattooing, and they regarded tattoos as a sign of beauty and a way of carrying memories. In Glitch Feminism, the author describes skin as a container. “It is a peel that contains and cradles wildness. It gives shape to bodies. A break, tear, rupture, or cut in skin opens a portal and passageway. Here, too, is both a world and a wound. Skin is both open and closed. Its presence suggests permanence, a border not meant to be crossed...It releases fluids and, at the same time, retains them.”

In the context of Glitch Feminism, this portrayal of skin becomes even more captivating, as it embodies the essence of our newly born digital existence. Drawing from a female perspective, I seek to carry the memories of the past and channel them into our digital lives, bridging history with the present.

This project aims to create a digital platform situated on social media that preserves the Nüshu language, explores its cultural context, and promotes feminist ideals.As we explore this uncharted territory of digital life, we are presented with an opportunity to open a gate—a gateway that connects not only the physical world but also the digital realm. It is a gate that allows our experiences, thoughts, and emotions to flow freely, unbound by traditional constraints.
